Senior year research paper

In AP Lit, we all wrote research papers connecting a thread of Mary Shelley's Frankenstein to a relevant piece of the modern world. Beyond that, the requirements were few and far between; the most notable was us needing to include at least two multimodal aspects. For my theme, I chose to connect marriages and close relationships in Frankenstein to the changes in marriage in today's society. The original assignment was submitted through Google Docs; I also reformatted it with LaTeX.

Junior year research paper

In AP Lang, we all wrote a 3-4,000 word research paper about a bill presented to the Congress, arguing for or against its passage. I chose H.R. 792 of the 119th Congress, which would add Trump's face to Mount Rushmore. I argued against its passage (though the bill died in committee). The original paper was submitted on Google Docs, though I reformatted it using LaTeX for this.

Sophomore year research paper

In our sophomore english classes, we wrote a 3-4,000 word research paper about some facet of contemporary society and its potential effects for the future. I chose to write about book bans. I focused more on the legal and constitutional parts of the debacle (though I only got so deep as a sophomore). While we were told to remain relatively unbiases, I did not do an excellent job at that. The original paper was submitted on Google Docs.
